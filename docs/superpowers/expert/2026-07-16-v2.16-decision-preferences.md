## 3. Findings — ranked by severity

Each finding = mechanism (cited) → concrete failure scenario → spec change. Mapped to the review's questions.

### 🔴 F-A (Q2) — The "why" capture is structurally a confabulation generator; "confirm a candidate" is NOT honest

**Mechanism.** Two independently-corroborated results converge here:
- **Choice blindness** (Johansson & Hall): people fluently confabulate *reasons for choices they did not even
  make* — in the supermarket-jam study, after their choice was covertly switched, subjects produced "elaborate
  explanations for why the [jam] they didn't choose was in fact the better one" ([LUCS](https://www.lucs.lu.se/fileadmin/user_upload/lucs/2011/01/Johansson-et-al.-2006-How-Something-Can-Be-Said-About-Telling-More-Than-We-Can-Know.pdf)).
  Confabulated reasons "seem completely plausible."
- **Rationale-driven preference manufacturing** (RLHF ≠ genuine preferences, [arXiv 2604.03238](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.03238)):
  requiring an annotator to justify/label a choice "may actually *generate* preferences rather than reveal
  them. The act of articulating reasoning can cement preferences that didn't previously exist" (fetch
  summary; builds on Slovic construction + Krosnick satisficing).
- **Acquiescence / satisficing** ([YouGov](https://yougov.com/articles/45308-how-leading-questions-and-acquiescence-bias-can-im), [Sage](https://methods.sagepub.com/ency/edvol/encyclopedia-of-survey-research-methods/chpt/acquiescence-response-bias)):
  people agree with a presented statement "when in doubt," *particularly with low prior information*, to
  minimize effort. Tapping a pre-written candidate is maximally low-effort.

Presenting ≥2 candidate rationales to *tap* is precisely the leading-question setup these results warn about.
The spec's own anti-ruflo rule says "only the human's confirmed answer is stored — never a fabricated
rationale." But choice-blindness shows a *human-confirmed* rationale can still be a fabrication the human
didn't originate. **The confirm step does not launder it.** A tapped candidate is a system-authored reason
wearing a human's rubber-stamp — the ruflo the project charter forbids, with a "human-CONFIRMED" badge on it.

**Failure scenario.** 11pm. Oleg picks "safe default + opt-in." The system offers candidate whys: *"safer
default; user owns the risk"* / *"less surprising behavior."* He taps the first — it sounds right, and typing
his own is effort (satisficing). Repeat across 4 similar forks. `preferences.md` now reads: *"Oleg values
'user owns the risk decision' — confirmed ×4."* He never reasoned that way; he tapped the least-effort
plausible option each time. The distilled model now steers future forks toward a value he never held, and it
is indistinguishable in the store from a genuine one.

**Spec change (MUST).**
1. Default why-capture is **UNPROMPTED free-text** or explicit skip → `why:null / why_source:none`.
2. Candidate rationales **MUST NOT be shown before** the human has produced-or-declined their own reason.
3. If candidates are offered *after* a free-text attempt, they are labeled *"not your words — rationales from
   earlier forks, for reference,"* and a tapped candidate is stored as a **third, weaker class**
   `why_source: "borrowed"` (distinct from `confirmed`/`edited`).
4. **Borrowed whys are excluded from the distilled dominant-rationale display** in `preferences.md` — they may
   be listed as "reference," never as "your rationale."
5. New AC: *a why produced by tapping a system-supplied candidate is never rendered as the human's rationale;
   a fixture asserts a borrowed why does not appear in the confirmed-rationale cluster.*

### 🔴 F-B (Q1) — Pre-select is a default nudge; even the dated evidence line anchors. The evidence default is safer but not neutral

**Mechanism.**
- **Constructed preference** (Slovic/Lichtenstein): people "do not possess complete preference orderings
  waiting to be revealed … they build preferences on the spot" from whatever is salient
  ([Cambridge](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/construction-of-preference/994FE8DFB8D431338B2A009F25271FBC),
  [Decision Research](https://www.decisionresearch.org/book-collection/the-construction-of-preference)).
  So "you usually pick X [dated]" is not a neutral reminder — it injects the most salient anchor into the
  construction process and **manufactures consistency with a past self** rather than eliciting present judgment.
- **Default effect** ([UX Magazine](https://uxmag.com/articles/the-psychology-of-defaults-how-pre-selected-options-influence-behavior),
  [Sue Behavioural Design](https://www.suebehaviouraldesign.com/en/blog/defaults-explained/)): the pre-selected
  option wins overwhelmingly (retirement enrollment 49% → 86% on which box was pre-checked) via status-quo
  bias + loss aversion + cognitive-effort avoidance. A highlighted "the option you'd pick" reframes the fork
  as *"be consistent with past-me vs. justify a deviation."*
- **Regulatory signal** ([GDPR Recital 32](https://www.cookieyes.com/blog/dark-patterns-in-cookie-consent/);
  [EU dark-patterns](https://cbtw.tech/insights/illegal-dark-patterns-europe/)): "Silence, pre-ticked boxes or
  inactivity" do not constitute consent; preselection is a named dark pattern *because* pre-selection ≠ free
  choice. This is not a compliance obligation for internal tooling, but it is the field's settled judgment
  that pre-select is manipulative by default.
- **Frame sensitivity** ([Framing the Default, PMC11612645](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11612645/)):
  the *presentation frame* itself moves the size of the default effect — so wording the evidence line is not
  cosmetic.

The spec's mitigations (evidence-default, gating, demotable) *reduce* but do not *remove* the nudge: even the
dated evidence line, shown adjacent to the live options at decision time, is an anchor. **The informing/leading
line is crossed the moment the past choice sits next to the live options rather than being pulled on demand.**

**Failure scenario.** Even in the default `evidence` mode, the fork shows *"prior 4/5 similar forks → X; you
said 'safer default' [2026-07-14]."* Oleg now chooses between "consistent with past-me" and "justify a
deviation I can't fully articulate at 11pm." He picks X — not because it's right for *this* fork, but because
deviating feels like it needs a reason. The evidence line manufactured a consistency that isn't fresh
judgment. No `preselect` mode was ever enabled; the "safe default" still nudged.

**Spec change (MUST/SHOULD).**
1. **MUST** phrase the evidence line as *falsifiable history that may not apply*, never as identity:
   ✅ *"On 4 past forks you chose X — those forks may differ from this one; decide this one on its merits."*
   ❌ *"you usually pick X"* / *"you value X."*
2. **SHOULD** make evidence **pull, not push** by default: recall shows a single collapsed affordance
   (*"🔎 4 related past decisions — reveal"*) and the anchor enters only when the human opens it. Add an
   `evidence-on-demand` config level and make it the recommended default for novel/high-stakes forks.
   New ladder: `off | evidence-on-demand | evidence | preselect`.
3. **MUST** never render the highlight as a checked/selected control (a literal pre-tick). It is an
   annotation the human must actively select; a fast-accept must not be silently counted as agreement
   (see F-C).
4. **SHOULD** require `preselect` mode to **re-arm per session/fork**, not persist as a standing setting —
   the same reason pre-ticked consent is invalid: a highlighted option should never be the resting state.
5. Given F-A + F-B + F-C together, **strongly consider cutting `preselect` from v1 entirely** and shipping
   evidence-on-demand only; pre-select is the highest-hazard, lowest-marginal-value part of the design.

### 🔴 F-C (Q3) — The anti-echo-chamber math is dark in the default mode, and the ceiling is measured on a contaminated signal

**Mechanism.**
- **Feedback-loop / lock-in**: recommender loops "continually reinforce" preferences and "narrow the range"
  over time ([TheSAI echo-chambers](https://thesai.org/Downloads/Volume16No10/Paper_71-Understanding_Echo_Chambers_in_Recommender_Systems.pdf)).
  The **Lock-in Hypothesis** ([arXiv 2506.06166](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.06166)) states the mechanism
  exactly for a human↔model loop: *"models learn human beliefs from data, reinforce these beliefs with
  generated content, reabsorb the reinforced beliefs, and feed them back to users again and again,"* observing
  "sudden and sustained drops in diversity." This design is that loop: capture → distill → recall → highlight
  → capture.
- **Automation complacency / learned helplessness** ([UXmatters 2026](https://www.uxmatters.com/mt/archives/2026/06/designing-for-doubt-how-to-prevent-automation-complacency-in-ai-workflows.php),
  [Springer 2025](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02422-7)): "if the dashboard rewards
  acceptance, the workforce will learn acceptance"; users defer to the machine even when their own judgment
  differs, and agency decays.

Three concrete defects in the design's anti-echo math:
- **(i) The metric is undefined in the default mode.** `override_rate = overrides / preselects_shown` only has
  a denominator when `preselect` is ON. In the default `evidence` mode nothing is "shown as preselect," so the
  demotion mechanism is **dark** — a pattern can ossify via the evidence-line anchor (F-B) and never trip the
  ceiling.
- **(ii) A "match" is a contaminated signal.** Counting a matched pick as a (weighted-less) confirming data
  point still feeds the loop, and a match may be complacency, not agreement. Worse: as learned helplessness
  sets in, Oleg *stops overriding*, so `override_rate` **drops** — which the system reads as "healthy, stable
  pattern," the exact inversion of the truth. Low override-rate is ambiguous: strong genuine agreement OR
  abandonment. The design cannot tell them apart.
- **(iii) 0.4 is a magic number over all-time.** A pattern with 20 historical confirms needs 8 overrides to
  demote — a stale taste is extremely sticky. The spec *wants* "a genuine change of mind moves it fastest,"
  but a lifetime ratio does the opposite of that.

**Failure scenarios.**
(a) *Evidence-mode ossification:* 6 forks, evidence line shown each time, Oleg anchors toward the shown past
choice each time; the pattern strengthens; `override_rate` is undefined; no demotion ever fires.
(b) *Learned helplessness:* after 15 confirms Oleg's taste has actually shifted, but overriding feels
pointless/effortful, so he keeps tapping the highlight. `override_rate` sits at ~0.1. `preferences.md` reports
a "strong, stable" pattern that is in fact dead.

**Spec change (MUST).**
1. **Track a drift signal in BOTH modes.** Define `agreement = (chosen == recalled_dominant)` on *every*
   recalled fork regardless of mode; demotion triggers on a **recency-weighted disagreement rate over the last
   K forks**, not an all-time ratio. This is how the spec's stated goal ("change of mind moves it fastest")
   is actually implemented.
2. **Add a staleness trip independent of override.** If a pattern has not been re-confirmed in > T days OR
   > M forks, auto-demote it to evidence-only regardless of `override_rate` — breaks the
   low-override-because-abandoned trap.
3. **Add an active-doubt holdout probe.** Every Kth qualifying fork, **suppress** the highlight/evidence and
   record the unprompted choice as a clean, uncontaminated signal. If the suppressed-fork choice diverges from
   the shown pattern, the pattern is stale/held-by-inertia. This is the recommender-systems "exploration" fix
   for feedback loops, adapted to a single user.
4. **Document 0.4 (and N, T, M, K) as conservative heuristics, not measured constants** — no fabricated
   confidence — and make them config-tunable.

### 🟡 F-D (Q3b) — Preference recall and Trigger-0 recon pull in opposite directions at the SAME forks; the "aligned, not against" claim is rhetorical, not structural

**Mechanism.** The project's own KB establishes anchoring/design-fixation as the *headline* risk and Trigger-0
as the countermeasure that **widens** a brainstorm (facts + ≥2–3 divergent directions, never converge) —
`research-grounded-ideation.md`: *"the first idea drops an anchor; most people circle the anchor and never
venture down the other, unexplored paths."* A preference lane inherently **narrows** toward past choices. The
spec's Global Constraints assert it is "aligned with Trigger-0, not against it" because recall shows the past
choice as "ONE dated input beside the live options." That is a *rhetorical* reconciliation — by the very
fixation mechanism the KB documents, a past-choice highlight at a fork **is** an anchor. On a fork Trigger-0
specifically spent tokens to widen, surfacing "you picked X before" **undoes the widening.**

**Failure scenario.** Trigger-0 recon on a novel fork surfaces divergent architectures A/B/C, explicitly
labeled "non-exhaustive — generate alternatives." The preference lane then highlights *"prior 4/5 similar forks
→ A."* The divergence the recon manufactured collapses back to A. The two features cancel — and the more
expensive one (recon) loses.

**Spec change (MUST).** Add an explicit suppression rule: on any fork that is **(a) recon-touched this
session**, or **(b) classified high-novelty / low-similarity to past decisions** (the FTS5 match score is
already computed — gate on it), preferences run **evidence-on-demand-only or fully suppressed, never
preselect.** The recon anti-anchoring header and preference recall MUST NOT both fire on the same fork. New
AC: *a recon-touched fork never receives a `pre_select`; a low-similarity match returns evidence-on-demand at
most.*

### 🟡 F-E (Q4) — A persisted "judgment model of a person" in a git repo needs bounded consent, dating, expiry, and one-command purge — the digital-twin minimum bar

**Mechanism.** Digital-twin / predictive-person literature: static representations "may not reflect evolving
user preferences over time" ([Quirks](https://www.quirks.com/articles/expanding-informed-consent-in-the-age-of-synthetic-data-and-digital-twins)),
consent must be *bounded* and *contextual* ([Petrie-Flom](https://petrieflom.law.harvard.edu/2025/10/29/predictive-persons-privacy-law-and-digital-twins/)),
and the field's guardrail model (Stanford) = the person **owns the copy, has an audit log, and can withdraw
consent** ([IBM/Stanford writeup via search](https://www.ibm.com/think/news/ai-simulations-stanford-research)),
under a "Minimally Viable Permissibility Principle" of consent + transparency + harm-mitigation + contextual
integrity ([TechPolicy.Press](https://www.techpolicy.press/digital-twins-demand-a-new-social-contract/)).

The spec stores `decisions.jsonl` + `preferences.md` as **git-tracked** prose under
`docs/superpowers/preferences/`. That means "Oleg's judgment model" travels with every clone, fork, PR, and
public release of the plugin. The anti-goals say "no cross-user model, single maintainer's taste" — but
**git distribution IS cross-user egress**: anyone who clones the repo gets a dated dossier of the maintainer's
decision patterns and confirmed rationales. That is a contextual-integrity problem the spec does not address.
There is direct in-project precedent: **v2.6.2** stopped committing machine-local personal fields into
`.claude/compound-v.json` after a downstream reviewer flagged them as gitignore-worthy — the same principle
applies, more strongly, to a psychological profile.

**Failure scenario.** The plugin is open-source. A contributor clones it and finds
`docs/superpowers/preferences/preferences.md` = *"Oleg: on safety-vs-power forks consistently chooses
safe-default; rationale 'user owns the risk' [dated]."* That is a distributed, publicly-readable behavioral
profile of the maintainer, outliving its accuracy and usable to predict/steer his choices in future PRs.
Nobody consented to *publishing* it; it shipped as a side effect of "it's the recall substrate, so it's
git-tracked."

**Spec change (MUST).**
1. **Decide egress explicitly.** EITHER gitignore `decisions.jsonl` + `preferences.md` as local-only (the
   v2.6.2-consistent choice — machine-local personal data stays out of the committed tree), OR add an
   explicit, documented consent notice that committing them publishes a personal judgment model. If they must
   be committed for the V-memory FTS5 lane, scope them to a path the release/marketplace build excludes.
2. **Every pattern carries `first_seen` + `last_confirmed` + an explicit expiry.** `/v:preferences` MUST offer
   a one-command **purge (forget-me)** and per-pattern prune (the "withdraw consent / own your copy" bar).
3. **Render each pattern as dated "what you did," with age prominent** and a *"stale — last confirmed N months
   ago"* banner past a threshold.
4. **Extend the secret-scan gate to PII in the free-text `why`** (the why field is human free-text and can
   contain names/emails/paths — refuse or redact, same fail-closed posture as the credential scan).

### 🟢 F-F (Q5) — Steelman that the feature is net-negative, and the conditions that flip it net-positive

**The net-negative case (steelmanned).** This project's stated moat (per its own memory: cross-vendor
verification, anti-anchoring recon, the "evidence never authority" firewall) is built on **resisting
convergence** — pulling in an outside model (Codex/Sol) precisely to challenge the creator's blind spots. A
"reason as the creator would" lane is the *antithesis* of that moat: it entrenches the single maintainer's
blind spots and suppresses the divergent challenge the project prizes. Three compounding problems:
- The "creator's judgment" being modeled may not be a stable object at all — constructed-preference theory
  says it is built per-decision, so the model is partly **fitting noise and calling it taste** (Slovic).
- The stored rationales are partly **manufactured** (F-A) and the confirm signal is partly **complacency**
  (F-C) — so the model optimizes *measurable* preferences, not genuine ones ([arXiv 2604.03238](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.03238)).
- The capture→distill→recall→capture loop is the **Lock-in Hypothesis** loop, which empirically produces
  "sudden and sustained drops in diversity" ([arXiv 2506.06166](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.06166)).

Net: a brainstorm that reasons "as Oleg would" produces **more Oleg, not better designs** — and adds a
psychological-profiling liability (F-E). Better designs come from the cross-model challenge this project
already has.

**What flips it net-positive.** The feature is defensible **only as a falsifiable-memory aid, not a judgment
model.** It is net-positive if it:
1. surfaces past decisions strictly **on demand** as searchable history (a decision-scoped `/v:remember`),
2. **never pre-selects** and **never renders a "you value X" identity claim** (only dated "you chose X on
   these forks"),
3. is paired with a **mandatory counter-move**: whenever it surfaces a past choice it ALSO surfaces the
   strongest *divergent* alternative (reuse the existing cross-model/red-team hook) — so recall triggers
   *challenge*, not convergence, and
4. treats every distilled pattern as a **hypothesis to falsify**, with the active-doubt holdout probe (F-C).

In one line: keep it as **dated evidence for the human's own recall + a trigger for divergence**; kill the
pre-select and the modeled-rationale, and the net flips positive.

## 4. Common Traps in This Domain

- **Trap A — "Human-confirmed" ≠ "genuine."** Choice blindness proves a human will confirm a reason that
  isn't theirs. A confirm click does not launder a system-authored rationale into fact.
- **Trap B — Treating a pre-select as neutral because "the human still taps."** The default effect operates
  *precisely* on options the user is free to change; freedom-to-override is what pre-selection exploits, not
  what excuses it.
- **Trap C — Measuring drift only where you nudge.** An anchor (the evidence line) that fires in a mode where
  the demotion metric is undefined ossifies invisibly.
- **Trap D — Low override-rate read as health.** It is ambiguous with abandonment/learned-helplessness; a
  feedback-loop system needs an *exploration/holdout* signal, not just a compliance rate.
- **Trap E — "It's the single maintainer's taste, so no privacy surface."** Git distribution is egress; a
  committed personal model is a distributed behavioral profile.
- **Trap F — Reconciling two opposing forces with prose.** "Widen" and "narrow" firing on the same fork is a
  structural conflict; the fix is a suppression rule, not a paragraph asserting alignment.

## 5. Regulatory / Compliance Notes

No hard statutory obligation — this is internal developer tooling with a single, self-modeling user, no
third-party PII by design. Two soft-but-load-bearing notes:
- **Preselection is the textbook regulated dark pattern.** [GDPR Recital 32](https://www.cookieyes.com/blog/dark-patterns-in-cookie-consent/)
  ("pre-ticked boxes … do not constitute consent") and the [EU dark-patterns regime](https://cbtw.tech/insights/illegal-dark-patterns-europe/)
  exist because pre-selection ≠ free choice. Not binding here, but it is the field's settled verdict that the
  `preselect` mode is manipulative-by-default — which is why it must be opt-in, re-armed, and never a literal
  pre-tick (F-B).
- **Distributing a personal model is a contextual-integrity question**, and the digital-twin norm is
  bounded/withdrawable consent + ownership + audit ([Petrie-Flom](https://petrieflom.law.harvard.edu/2025/10/29/predictive-persons-privacy-law-and-digital-twins/),
  [TechPolicy.Press](https://www.techpolicy.press/digital-twins-demand-a-new-social-contract/)). The egress
  decision in F-E is the compliance-shaped call.

## 7. Design Constraints for the Plan (non-negotiable)

1. **Why-capture is unprompted free-text (or explicit skip → null) by default.** Candidate rationales are
   never shown before the human's own attempt; a tapped candidate is stored as a weaker `why_source:"borrowed"`
   and excluded from the confirmed-rationale display. (F-A)
2. **The evidence line is dated, falsifiable history that may not apply — never an identity claim** ("you
   chose X on these forks," never "you value X" / "you usually pick X"). (F-B)
3. **Add an `evidence-on-demand` config level and make it the recommended default; evidence is pull, not
   push, for novel/high-stakes forks.** Ladder: `off | evidence-on-demand | evidence | preselect`. (F-B)
4. **The highlight is never a literal pre-tick / pre-selected control; a fast-accept is not silently counted
   as agreement.** `preselect` re-arms per session/fork. Strongly consider cutting `preselect` from v1. (F-B)
5. **Drift is tracked in ALL modes** via `agreement = chosen == recalled_dominant` on every recalled fork;
   demotion is a **recency-weighted, last-K disagreement rate**, not an all-time override ratio. (F-C)
6. **A staleness trip auto-demotes a pattern not re-confirmed in > T days / > M forks, independent of
   override-rate.** (F-C)
7. **An active-doubt holdout probe suppresses the highlight every Kth fork and records the clean choice**;
   divergence flags the pattern stale. (F-C)
8. **All numeric thresholds (N, 0.4, T, M, K) are documented conservative heuristics, config-tunable, never
   presented as measured confidence.** (F-C)
9. **Preferences are suppressed (or evidence-on-demand only, never preselect) on any recon-touched or
   high-novelty/low-similarity fork.** Recon-widen and preference-narrow never both fire on one fork. (F-D)
10. **Egress is decided explicitly: local-only (gitignored, v2.6.2-consistent) OR committed-with-consent**;
    if committed for FTS5, excluded from the release/marketplace build. (F-E)
11. **Each pattern carries first_seen + last_confirmed + expiry; `/v:preferences` offers one-command purge
    and per-pattern prune; stale patterns are visibly banner-flagged.** (F-E)
12. **The secret-scan gate extends to PII in the free-text `why`.** (F-E)
13. **Whenever recall surfaces a past choice, it also surfaces the strongest divergent alternative** (reuse the
    cross-model/red-team hook) — recall triggers challenge, not convergence. (F-F)

## 8. Open Questions for the Human (product/Oleg must decide — I will not fabricate these)

1. **What is the feature actually for — "help me recall my own past reasoning" or "let the brainstorm reason
   as me"?** These are different products. The first is defensible as falsifiable memory (F-F); the second is
   the hazardous one (entrenches blind spots, manufactures a false self-consistency, builds the Lock-in loop).
   The whole audit's severity hinges on this answer.
2. **Egress:** local-only (gitignored) or committed-with-consent? v2.6.2 precedent points to local-only; the
   FTS5-indexing requirement pushes the other way. Product call. (F-E)
3. **Keep `preselect` mode in v1 at all?** Given F-A/F-B/F-C, my recommendation is evidence-on-demand only for
   v1; confirm whether the pre-select value justifies its manipulation surface.
4. **Numeric thresholds** (N, override ceiling, staleness T/M, holdout cadence K): I will not fabricate these.
   Set them as conservative, documented, tunable defaults.
5. **Is there any fork class where anchoring on past taste is actually *desired*** (e.g. hard house-style
   conventions that *should* be sticky)? If so, that is a separate, explicitly-scoped "conventions" lane, not
   the judgment model — confirm it is not being conflated with taste.
